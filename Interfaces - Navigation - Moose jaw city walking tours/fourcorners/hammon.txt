hammond=The Hammond was erected by the Canadian City and Town Properties, which was formed in Liverpool in 1910.  The architects for the building were Atchison from Winnipeg and Troy Owens of the Canadian City and Town Properties.  Construction started April 22, 1912.
Four stories were to be built at once with two stories to be added later.  (These were never built).  It features an ornate cornice and elaborate brickwork.  The ground floor is covered with decorative glazed terra cotta, one of the best examples of this in Western Canada.
